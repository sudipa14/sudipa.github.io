Research Experience

INTERACTION BASED CREDIBILITY ANALYSIS OF NEWS ON FACEBOOK USING MACHINE LEARNING METHODOLOGIES

Supervisor: Dr.Sadia Sharmin

Fake news and its dissemination are growing more popular as social media becomes more pervasive in our daily lives and Facebook users are particularly vulnerable. Numerous studies on detecting fake news have already been published. However, a study focusing on fake news in low resource languages like Bengali propagated through Facebook is rare due to data extraction challenges. In this paper, we work with fake news in the Bengali language and used various supervised machine learning algorithms to classify Facebook posts as fake, real, or satire and found XGBoost to generate the best outputs. Instead of the content, we trained our model with the interaction data of the posts to make it resilient against adversarial attacks. We observed that ensemble methods perform well with such social media metrics. This work was accepted in SITIS - 2022.

Exploring Impacts of Environmental Exposure on Human Beings

Supervisor: Dr.A.B.M Alim Al Islam

People in developing or underdeveloped countries such as Bangladesh are always subjected to harsh environmental conditions with little to no measures taken against these harsh conditions. Through our work we reflect on the impacts of environmental exposure upon human physiology, psychology, etc. within diversified communities such as rickshaw pullers, beggars, daily workers, etc. and so on. We want to look for ways that can tell us about exploring environmental exposure on a person in the most natural setting involving little effort from the person being studied. This work is accepted in NSysS-2024.
